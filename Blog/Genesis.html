Genesis

In the beginning, there is only light. Things have only gone downhill since.

The above sentence summarizes the "Genesis" chapter of the bible. Even though god created everything to be holy, mankind succumbed to their temptation and stole a fruit from the sacred tree. Interestingly enough, the fruit grants mankind the ability to differentiate between "good and evil", or in a more general sense, knowledge. However, it is exactly this "knowledge of good and evil" that expelled Adam and Eve from Eden.

This line of thought is better explored in Paradise Lost by Milton, whereas the effects of the "fruit of good and evil" are more apparent, giving mankind feelings of lust, envy, and guilt. It is then elaborated upon that through "gaining knowledge" of these sinful emotions, humans bore the original sin, causing their expulsion from Eden. From a more meta point of view, when man are in their most naive states, they live a sinless life that is devoid of knowledge, but as they mature and grow, they obtain knowledge but become tainted by sin in the process. However, some would argue that this state of "sin" from knowledge would still be inherently more meaningful than a carefree but unexamined life in Eden.

Today is also the Genesis of this blog and the genesis of my adult life(somewhat). This summer marked many firsts: the first time in my life working full time, my first summer after college, my first time learning how to drive, and the list goes on. With new experiences comes new knowledge, and a new understanding of myself.

Sometimes I think back to my elementary school days and just reflect on how carefree life was. I was certainly less stressed back then. However, I would probably not trade away what I currently have to live a carefree life. Being an adult means taking on responsibility, but with responsibility comes the freedom to realize one's ambition. I am really happy to be in a position where I can work hard towards a goal that I ultimately really desire.

To the end of my childhood, and the genesis of my adulthood.
